paragraph is a distinct, self-contained section of writing that develops a single unified idea or topic. It typically begins on a new line and visually acts as a building block for longer texts, essays, or documents.Essential Components of a Standard ParagraphTopic Sentence: The opening sentence that introduces the paragraph's main point or controlling idea.Supporting Sentences: The middle sentences that provide evidence, examples, details, or explanations to back up the topic sentence.Concluding Sentence: The final sentence that summarizes the point, offers a resolution, or bridges the idea to the next paragraph.Types of ParagraphsIntroductory: Establishes the main thesis or argument of a larger piece of work.
